We turn to visualizations to bring data to life because people are naturally better at making sense of visuals than they are at digesting mountains of numbers in rows and columns.

As we become increasingly data-driven in our decision making and the volume of data in our environment grows, easy ways to see patterns and predict future outcomes from that data are more important. We rely on visualizations to make sense of information, but also to tell stories about the information.

Users need to know how and when to use heat maps, scatter/bubble charts, tree maps, Sankey diagrams and waterfall charts. Each offers an additional way to navigate through and communicate the findings within data beyond that available in bar, line, map and pie. The process to create and modify various ad hoc data visualization from datasets so as to answer questions as a journey is the key to getting value from it, especially for self-service users.
