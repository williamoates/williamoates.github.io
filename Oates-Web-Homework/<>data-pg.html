Interactive data visualization has become widely used since Gartner first wrote about it almost a decade ago. Not just within BI, but also in the media and on smartphone and tablet apps. As technology consumers, we're now used to websites that use filters and selectors to examine and navigate charts on all kinds of data, from our banking transactions to the performance of our favorite sports people and teams. We're used to lassoing, panning, zooming and revisualizing data on charts and maps.
